About Goddess Baglamukhi

Goddess Baglamukhi, also known as Pitambara Devi, is the 8th Mahavidya among the ten Mahavidyas in Hinduism. She is the goddess of power, wisdom, and victory. Her name literally means "the one who restrains the tongue," and she is worshipped to paralyze enemies, destroy evil forces, and gain control over adversaries.

She is typically depicted with a golden complexion, wearing yellow clothes, and seated on a throne in the middle of a golden throne. Her worship is most effective when performed during the night of Amavasya (new moon).

Devotees seek her blessings to overcome legal issues, defeat enemies, remove black magic or negativity, and achieve spiritual peace.
